# AI-Assisted UI/UX for an Autonomous Agent Software Factory — Research Report

**Date:** 2026-06-22
**Audience:** Main Architect (implementation), founder (decisions in plain language at the end)
**Question:** How do strong software teams produce high-quality, tasteful, consistent, easily-modifiable UI/UX *with the help of AI* — what tools, workflows, "skills," and methodologies — and how do we adapt these to our autonomous multi-agent factory building a Romanian back-office ERP (Django + React/TypeScript), where no human writes UI and nothing currently *looks* at the rendered screen before it is declared "done"?

**Method note / honesty:** Findings are drawn from primary sources (vendor docs, design-system docs, NN/g, W3C, peer-reviewed arXiv papers, and named engineering blogs). Inline URLs are given for every load-bearing claim. Where a claim is vendor marketing, unmeasured practitioner folklore, or where sources disagree, it is flagged. Where a tool is hype vs proven, that judgment is explicit. This report cross-references our own canon: `_FRAMEWORK_MVP_DoD.md` (the `SPEC → BUILD → VALIDATE → [AUDIT] → MERGE GATE → DONE` pipeline), `docs/projects/erp/PROJECT.md` (UX-first law, risk classes), `ERP-start/docs/Business/domain/technical-context.md` (founder's UI/UX requirements), and the live frontend in `erp-workspace/frontend/src/ui/` (18 `App*` wrappers + one `theme/tokens.ts`).

## 1. Executive Summary

**The core finding, stated once.** Across vendors *and* independent engineering sources, the mid-2026 consensus is identical and it lands squarely on our doctrine of *mechanical guarantees over attention*: **AI reliably writes the first 60–80% of a UI, but quality, consistency, and taste are produced by mechanical constraint plus a human visual gate — never by prompting or by hoping the agent has taste.** Every primary source agrees that advisory instructions (prose in a rules file, "please use our components") degrade or get silently dropped, while a failing check does not. ([Thoughtworks Radar v34](https://www.thoughtworks.com/about-us/news/2026/combat-ai-cognitive-debt-radar-v34); [Builder.io](https://www.builder.io/blog/how-to-make-ai-agents-follow-your-design-system); [Anthropic best-practices](https://code.claude.com/docs/en/best-practices))

This maps to three conclusions for us:

1. **Our "tasteless/inconsistent" problem is structurally predictable, and largely mechanically fixable.** AI UI drifts because the model "defaults to whatever patterns appear most frequently in its training set" and has no memory across components — by the fifth screen you get "a patchwork of inconsistent decisions that no amount of 'make it look professional' can fix" ([Puck](https://puckeditor.com/blog/ai-slop-vs-constrained-ui); [braingrid](https://www.braingrid.ai/blog/design-system-optimized-for-ai-coding)). The fixes are the ones we have *half*-built: a fenced component barrel, design tokens as a hard contract, and — the parts we are missing — **lint rules that fail the build on raw values/illegal imports**, an **enriched UX-discovery spec gate**, a **screenshot self-check in the build agent**, and a **cheap human visual preview before sign-off**.

2. **The single biggest gap is that nothing ever *sees* the rendered screen.** Our quality is verified only by behavioral vitest (`getByRole`), confirmed by inspection of `erp-workspace/frontend/src` — zero screenshot, visual-regression, or vision checks exist. Two independent, cheap mechanisms close this: (a) an **agent screenshot self-check** (render → screenshot → rubric critique → fix, capped at 2–3 iterations) — proven to improve frontend-code quality by ~9–18% in controlled studies ([arXiv 2604.05839](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.05839v1)) — and (b) a **human visual preview gate** (a per-change live URL the founder clicks through), which every real autonomous team keeps as the hard line before merge ([Stripe Minions](https://stripe.dev/blog/minions-stripes-one-shot-end-to-end-coding-agents); [VentureBeat on v0](https://venturebeat.com/infrastructure/vercel-rebuilt-v0-to-tackle-the-90-problem-connecting-ai-generated-code-to)).

3. **Multimodal LLMs can detect *broken* UI but cannot certify *good* UI.** The evidence is unusually clear and split by task: VLMs are strong at structural/visibility defects (blank page, overflow, overlap, missing element) and **hallucination-prone on aesthetic/subjective judgment** — "MLLMs are not at a stage where they should be seen as replacements for real-life human evaluation" ([arXiv 2510.08783](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.08783v1)). Therefore the agent self-check is an *advisory tripwire for breakage*, and the *founder remains the sole taste arbiter* — exactly the division `technical-context.md` already encodes. For a **data-dense internal ERP**, "correct and not visibly broken" is most of the bar, so this limitation hurts us far less than it would a marketing site.

**What to adopt, in one breath (full table in §5):** enrich the spec into a **UX-discovery checklist gate**; **split frontend and backend into distinct build stages**; add **mechanical fences** (ESLint `no-restricted-imports` for the antd fence + a `color-no-hex`/strict-value token rule) wired into Tier-1; add a **build-agent screenshot self-check**; add a **cheap human visual preview** before phase sign-off; make the **design tokens config-driven** so the founder can re-theme centrally. **Skip / defer:** the closed SaaS app-builders (v0/Lovable/bolt/Figma Make) as anything more than optional scaffold-draft tools; Figma MCP/Code Connect (we have no Figma source and it is Org/Enterprise-gated); Chromatic/Percy (buy only if CI rendering flake becomes painful).

## 2. Prong A — AI-Assisted UI/UX Tools & Workflows (PRIMARY)

### 2.1 The design-to-code / UI-generation category — survey and fitness

The honest framing: **the closed SaaS "app builders" are greenfield prototype generators** that scaffold *their own* new project; they are the wrong *shape* for "an agent writes into our existing Django+React repo with an existing component library." The tools that actually target "map to my own components and open a PR into my existing repo" are a different, smaller set. ([wz-it comparison](https://wz-it.com/en/blog/lovable-vs-bolt-vs-v0-comparison-2026/) states bluntly that "none integrate into existing codebases" for the consumer builders.)

| Tool | What it is | Strength | Weakness / limit | Fit for us | Hype vs proven |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **v0 (Vercel)** | NL/Figma → React+TS+Tailwind+shadcn | Cleanest UI output of the builders; can import a repo; designed to coexist with Cursor/Claude Code ([v0.app/docs/faqs](https://v0.app/docs/faqs)) | Frontend only — "no built-in authentication system, no server-side business logic, no ORM" ([nxcode](https://www.nxcode.io/resources/news/v0-by-vercel-complete-guide-2026)); React/Next-only; outputs **shadcn/Tailwind, not Ant Design** unless forcefully fenced ([Vercel](https://vercel.com/blog/how-to-prompt-v0)) | Optional **draft/mockup** source only | PROVEN for UI generation; HYPE as "full-stack" |

**The decisive judgment ([wz-it](https://wz-it.com/en/blog/lovable-vs-bolt-vs-v0-comparison-2026/); [v0 docs](https://v0.app/docs/faqs)):** *none of these SaaS builders is the autonomous repo-resident agent we already have.* The honest architecture for "agent writes into our existing repo with our component library" is **a repo-native coding agent (which we have) fenced to our barrel** — optionally fed a UI draft by v0 or component-reuse by Builder/Anima as PR-generating assistants behind our existing merge gate. We do not need to buy an app-builder; we need to harden the agent + pipeline we already run. This is good news: it means the work is *configuration of our own factory*, not a vendor migration.

**Category-wide reality check (do not skip):** Veracode 2025 (100+ models) found ~45% of AI-generated code introduced OWASP-Top-10 issues; 2026 testing put v0/Lovable/bolt at a 40–45% vulnerability rate; realistic end-to-end speedups are ~10–15%, not the marketed 60–90% ([wz-it](https://wz-it.com/en/blog/lovable-vs-bolt-vs-v0-comparison-2026/); [sixtythirtyten](https://www.sixtythirtyten.co/blog/from-figma-to-code-ai-design-to-dev-workflows-in-2026)). Our existing Tier-1 + dual-audit + Tier-2 gates are exactly the "keep every generator behind a PR + review/validation gate" that the sources prescribe — we are structurally well-positioned, but only because we already gate; the gates must extend to UI-visual quality, which today they do not.

### 2.2 Why shadcn/ui pairs unusually well with AI coding agents (and what it means for our antd choice)

shadcn/ui inverts the normal library model. Instead of an opaque npm package an agent can only *call*, it **copies editable component source into your repo** ("This is not a component library. It is how you build your component library." — [ui.shadcn.com/docs](https://ui.shadcn.com/docs)). The docs name the consequence explicitly under "Open Code": **"AI Integration: Access to the code makes it straightforward for LLMs to read, understand, and even improve your components,"** and an "AI-Ready" section: "its open code and consistent API allow AI models to read, understand, and even generate new components" ([ui.shadcn.com/docs](https://ui.shadcn.com/docs)).

Four reinforcing layers make it agent-legible:
1. **Open code in-repo** — the agent opens the actual file, learns house style from siblings, edits in place ([docs](https://ui.shadcn.com/docs)).
2. **Inline Tailwind + semantic CSS-variable tokens** put style intent in the exact text the model edits; **Radix primitives** keep accessibility/keyboard/focus correct under restyling, so the agent can restyle freely without breaking a11y ([radix-ui.com/primitives](https://www.radix-ui.com/primitives)).
3. **A registry + CLI as a "distribution system for code"** — `shadcn add <name|url|@ns/name>` is one deterministic command; `components.json` `aliases` "place generated components in the correct location and rewrite imports"; you can publish a **private/namespaced registry** of your own components *and* ship an `AGENTS.md` through it ([cli docs](https://ui.shadcn.com/docs/cli); [registry](https://ui.shadcn.com/docs/registry/namespace)).
4. **An MCP server + an official agent "skill"** that read `components.json` and let the agent search/install components from natural language and "generate correct code on first attempt" ([mcp](https://ui.shadcn.com/docs/mcp); [skills](https://ui.shadcn.com/docs/skills)).

**Honest implication for our antd choice.** Our stack is Ant Design behind `App*` wrappers (ADR-0002), *not* shadcn. The shadcn advantages we **already capture** via our wrapper barrel: the wrappers are open source-in-repo the agent can read and imitate, and our `tokens.ts` is a single styling source. The advantages we **do not** capture: Tailwind's inline-utility legibility, the registry/MCP install ergonomics, and the agent skill. **This validates the existing planned shadcn migration path** (only the wrapper layer imports the base library) — but it is *not* the urgent lever. The urgent levers (mechanical fences, visual feedback) are library-agnostic and apply to antd today. **Recommendation: keep antd for now; the shadcn migration is a medium-term quality multiplier, not the fix for "tasteless."** (Caveat from the source: all shadcn tooling is frontend-only; it touches nothing in Django.)

### 2.3 Visual feedback loop — letting the agent SEE the rendered UI

This is our single biggest missing capability. The evidence:

- **The loop works and is measured.** "Vision-Guided Iterative Refinement for Frontend Code Generation" implements generate → render → VLM-critique → revise with a fixed **T=3** budget and a Claude vision critic: refinement *with* visual critique improved scores +6.2%→+8.7% (best retained intermediate +17.8%), while refinement *without* the visual signal gained only +1.5% — i.e. **the visual feedback is what does the work** ([arXiv 2604.05839](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.05839v1)). "ReLook" confirms the pattern with RL and adds a "strict zero-reward rule for invalid renders [to] prevent reward hacking," and notably **drops the external critic at inference** for a cheap capped self-edit (latency 123s→18s) ([arXiv 2510.11498](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.11498v1)).
- **Anthropic ships this as a first-class recommended pattern:** "[paste screenshot] implement this design. take a screenshot of the result and compare it to the original. list differences and fix them" — the screenshot check is what lets a run finish unattended, because "Claude stops when the work looks done. Without a check it can run, 'looks done' is the only signal" ([Anthropic best-practices](https://code.claude.com/docs/en/best-practices)).
- **The mechanism to drive a browser:** **Playwright MCP** (Microsoft, official) operates on the **accessibility tree by default** (`browser_snapshot`, ~200–400 tokens, "no vision models needed"), with `browser_take_screenshot`, `browser_console_messages`, click/type, ~30 tools; pixel clicking is opt-in via `--caps=vision` ([github.com/microsoft/playwright-mcp](https://github.com/microsoft/playwright-mcp)). **Chrome DevTools MCP** (Google, official) exists precisely because "coding agents... are not able to see what the code they generate actually does when it runs in the browser," exposing 31 tools incl. screenshots, console, network, Lighthouse ([github.com/ChromeDevTools/chrome-devtools-mcp](https://github.com/ChromeDevTools/chrome-devtools-mcp)).
- **The hard limit — VLMs judge structure, not taste.** Strong at fidelity: Design2Code found GPT-4V screenshot-to-code output judged "better than the original reference in 64% of cases" ([arXiv 2403.03163](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.03163)). Weak at aesthetics: across GPT-4o/Claude/Llama, exact-match agreement with human UI judgment was only ~35–38%, "almost all models suffer from... hallucination in aesthetic interpretation," and pairwise accuracy collapses to ~50% (random) when two UIs are *similar* ([arXiv 2510.08783](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.08783v1)). A multi-dimensional **rubric** judge agreed with humans 69.5% of the time vs only 48.5% for a single holistic score ([arXiv 2604.05839](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.05839v1)).

**Conclusion for our build agent:** add a **render → screenshot → closed-rubric critique → fix loop, capped at 2–3 iterations, retaining the best intermediate**, framed as yes/no checks ("blank? overlap? text overflow? off-screen control? expected section present? severe contrast issue?") — **not** a "is it pretty" score. Treat a flag as *escalate*, not auto-fail (false-negative rate on subtle layout is real). Use **Playwright MCP** as the driver (the a11y snapshot is itself a cheap, strong correctness signal: if expected roles/names are missing, the UI is broken regardless of pixels). This is an *advisory tripwire for breakage*; it is **not** a mechanical guarantee of good UI and must not be sold as one.

### 2.4 Visual-regression testing

The deterministic backbone that should sit *under* any VLM critique — it answers "did this change alter pixels we didn't intend?" with no model in the loop:

- **Playwright `toHaveScreenshot()`** (free, built-in): pixel diff with `maxDiffPixelRatio`/`threshold` tuning; auto-retries until the page stabilizes. **Flakiness control is the whole game** — disable animations, mask volatile regions (timestamps), freeze the clock, seed data, and **generate baselines in the same containerized env CI uses** (official Playwright Docker image) or every run flakes ([playwright.dev/docs/test-snapshots](https://playwright.dev/docs/test-snapshots)).
- **Storybook test-runner** (free): turns every story into a render smoke test (passes if it renders, fails if it errors) + interaction/a11y assertions ([storybook.js.org](https://storybook.js.org/docs/writing-tests/integrations/test-runner)). For a component-driven React/TS ERP this is the cheapest highest-value gate.
- **Chromatic** (Storybook team, paid SaaS): cloud-consistent rendering removes local-env flake; free tier Chrome-only 5,000 snaps/mo, paid from ~$149–179/mo; its human-approval UI review is **largely wasted in a no-human pipeline** ([chromatic.com/pricing](https://www.chromatic.com/pricing)). **Percy** (BrowserStack) is the generic-CI equivalent.

**Shared honest weakness:** pixel-diff detects *change*, not *correctness or quality* — "row 3 moved 4px," never "this screen is confusing." For us the cost-optimal pick is **Playwright `toHaveScreenshot` + Storybook test-runner (both free)**; buy Chromatic only if CI baseline flake becomes painful.

### 2.5 Figma Dev Mode MCP / Code Connect

Lets an agent read *structured design context* (not just a picture) from Figma: `get_code`, `get_variable_defs` (your tokens), `get_screenshot`, and `get_code_connect_map`. With **Code Connect** the agent emits *your actual components*; without it "the model is guessing" ([github.com/figma/mcp-server-guide](https://github.com/figma/mcp-server-guide)). **But:** authoring Code Connect requires **Org/Enterprise**, the free tier's ~6 MCP calls/month is unusable, and **we have no Figma source for the ERP** ([Figma rate limits](https://developers.figma.com/docs/figma-mcp-server/rate-limits-access/)). **Verdict: SKIP for now.** It is the single biggest reliability win *if* a design source exists ("match the spec" replaces "judge the aesthetics") — revisit only if we ever introduce Figma mockups.

## 3. Prong B — Established UI/UX Methodology for Data-Heavy Enterprise Apps (FOUNDATION)

This is the body of knowledge we encode into the spec gate (§4). Each item below is checklist-able.

### 3.1 Design systems, atomic design, tokens
- **Atomic design** (Brad Frost): atoms → molecules → organisms → templates → pages; "not a linear process" but "a mental model... a cohesive whole and a collection of parts at the same time"; building molecules/organisms "encourages reusability, and promotes consistency" ([atomicdesign.bradfrost.com/chapter-2](https://atomicdesign.bradfrost.com/chapter-2/)).
- **Why enterprises use design systems** (NN/g): "a complete set of standards intended to manage design at scale using reusable components and patterns" providing "a single source of components, patterns, and styles" ([nngroup.com/articles/design-systems-101](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/design-systems-101/)).
- **Ant Design** (our library) is explicitly "a design system for enterprise-level products"; its values are **Natural, Certain, Meaningful, Growing**, and it exposes a **Design Token** theming system ([ant.design/docs/spec/values](https://ant.design/docs/spec/values); [customize-theme](https://ant.design/docs/react/customize-theme)). IBM **Carbon** and **Material 3** corroborate the React-first, token-driven, "harvested patterns" approach ([carbondesignsystem.com](https://carbondesignsystem.com/all-about-carbon/what-is-carbon/); [m3.material.io](https://m3.material.io/foundations)).
- **Design tokens** (W3C DTCG, **first stable version v2025.10**): a token is a "name/value pair" in JSON with `$`-prefixed reserved props and **aliases** (`{token.name}`) for semantic references; the format exists for "interoperability between tools" ([designtokens.org/tr/drafts/format](https://www.designtokens.org/tr/drafts/format/); [w3.org stable announce](https://www.w3.org/community/design-tokens/2025/10/28/design-tokens-specification-reaches-first-stable-version/)). **Style Dictionary** transforms one JSON source into per-platform outputs — "a single source of truth" updated "in one location" ([styledictionary.com/info/dtcg](https://styledictionary.com/info/dtcg/)).

### 3.2 Information architecture & navigation (NN/g + design-system docs)
- **Sidebar/left-nav** for broad IAs (>~7–9 categories) and growing B2B/enterprise apps ([nngroup.com/articles/vertical-nav](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/vertical-nav/)); Ant Design: suits "multi-level, operation intensive and dashboard-like web apps," and **page depth "should ideally be lower than 3 and not exceed 5"** ([ant.design/docs/spec/navigation](https://ant.design/docs/spec/navigation/)). Keep nav permanently visible on desktop, full text labels, current item highest priority.
- **Tabs** only for clearly distinct groupings users won't compare side-by-side; **single row only** (stacking destroys spatial memory); 1–2 plain words ([nngroup.com/articles/tabs-used-right](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/tabs-used-right/)).
- **Command palette (⌘K)** is an *additive power-user layer — add only after basic navigation is strong*; show the shortcut next to each result ([uxpatterns.dev](https://uxpatterns.dev/patterns/advanced/command-palette) — flagged lower-authority, no NN/g article).
- **Role-based views:** distinguish system-driven *personalization* from user-driven *customization* (which "works poorly for busy professionals"); define roles by job function from existing identity data; **a sound base IA must come first** ([nngroup.com/articles/customization-personalization](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/customization-personalization/)). This directly informs our deferred per-user view-preferences mechanism.
- **General IA:** the "3-click rule" is false (information scent matters, not click count); progressive disclosure is **best at two levels, three+ gets users lost**; always answer "where am I?" with breadcrumbs + highlighted current location ([nngroup.com/articles/ia-study-guide](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ia-study-guide/); [progressive-disclosure](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/progressive-disclosure/)).

### 3.3 Enterprise interaction patterns (directly relevant to ERP screens)
- **Dense data tables** — design against **4 tasks: find, compare, view/edit/add one row, act on records**; **freeze header + leftmost identifier column**; **right-align numbers** (compared digit-by-digit), left-align text, tabular figures; multiple row densities (Carbon ships 5, Ant `size`); zebra striping + hover; first column a human-readable identifier not an auto-ID; inline actions scale to 1–2, beyond that use checkbox-select + batch bar; **avoid modals for deep row editing** (they cover reference rows) — prefer a non-modal side panel; virtualization for large sets ([nngroup.com/articles/data-tables](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/data-tables/); [carbondesignsystem.com/components/data-table/style](https://carbondesignsystem.com/components/data-table/style/); [ant.design/components/table](https://ant.design/components/table)). The numeric right-align rule is from secondary UX sources cross-checked against the NN/g scannability principle.
- **Complex forms** — **single-column** (multiple columns "interrupt the vertical momentum"); **labels above fields**, wired for screen readers; cluster related fields into sections; minimize + explicitly mark optional fields; **inline validation on blur, not mid-typing**; error messages next to the field with red + icon (not color alone), preserving input; avoid tooltip-only and modal errors ([nngroup.com/articles/web-form-design](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/web-form-design/); [errors-forms-design-guidelines](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/errors-forms-design-guidelines/)).
- **Filtering & search** — filter vs facet; search and filters are complementary; instant filtering for fast (<1s) data, **Apply-button (batch) for slow data / several criteria**; show applied filters as removable chips + a single "Clear all"; **don't auto-scroll results to top while the user is still filtering** ([nngroup.com/articles/filters-vs-facets](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/filters-vs-facets/); [applying-filters](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/applying-filters/); [carbondesignsystem.com/patterns/filtering](https://carbondesignsystem.com/patterns/filtering/)).
- **Master-data CRUD** — prefer **master-detail** with a non-modal edit panel; **confirm only serious/irreversible actions**, restate the specific consequence (never "Are you sure?"), label buttons with the action ("Delete account", not Yes/No), separate destructive (red) from cancel, **always provide Undo**; reserve type-to-confirm for high-severity deletes ([nngroup.com/articles/confirmation-dialog](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/confirmation-dialog/); [cloudscape](https://cloudscape.design/patterns/resource-management/delete/delete-with-additional-confirmation/)).
- **Inline creation ("+ Add new" from a picker)** — *flagged important; this is a recurring ERP need (create a counterparty/part from inside a document form).* Goal: add a missing value **without leaving the form**. **Ant Design Select is the canonical implementation** via `popupRender` (formerly `dropdownRender`) with a footer "+ Add item", or `mode="tags"` with `tokenSeparators`, or AutoComplete; create the record **without unmounting the form** and immediately select it; for long master lists make the picker searchable so "Add new" appears only after no match ([ant.design/components/select](https://ant.design/components/select/); [GH issue 7988](https://github.com/ant-design/ant-design/issues/7988)).

### 3.4 Discovery & evaluation methods (the spine of the UX-discovery gate)
- **Jobs-To-Be-Done:** users "hire" a product to make progress in a circumstance; every job has **functional, social, emotional** dimensions ([christenseninstitute.org](https://www.christenseninstitute.org/theory/jobs-to-be-done/)).
- **User-flow mapping:** "a set of interactions that describe the typical or ideal set of steps to accomplish a task"; for an ERP the per-task user flow is the right artifact ([nngroup.com/articles/user-journeys-vs-user-flows](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/user-journeys-vs-user-flows/)).
- **Nielsen's 10 heuristics** (canonical, [nngroup.com/articles/ten-usability-heuristics](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/ten-usability-heuristics/)): 1 visibility of system status; 2 match system & real world; 3 user control & freedom (undo); 4 consistency & standards; 5 error prevention; 6 recognition over recall; 7 flexibility & efficiency (expert accelerators); 8 aesthetic & minimalist design; 9 help users recover from errors; 10 help & documentation. NN/g's **complex-app guidance** adds: detailed progress indicators for long processes; version history/rollback; real-time preview during complex config; visual recognition cues so users needn't recall codes; defer rarely-used elements to a secondary level; inline context-sensitive help ([usability-heuristics-complex-applications](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/usability-heuristics-complex-applications/)).
- **Heuristic evaluation:** 3–5 evaluators independently, two passes, consolidate ([how-to-conduct-a-heuristic-evaluation](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/how-to-conduct-a-heuristic-evaluation/)) — *adaptable as an audit-agent rubric.*
- **Accessibility (WCAG):** W3C/WAI standard; **POUR** (Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust); levels A/AA/AAA, **AA is the common target** ([w3.org/WAI/standards-guidelines/wcag](https://www.w3.org/WAI/standards-guidelines/wcag/)).
- **Cross-screen consistency:** maintain internal *and* external consistency; inconsistency "erodes trust"; the tool to fight it is "regular design reviews flagging deviations from the standard" ([nngroup.com/articles/consistency-and-standards](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/consistency-and-standards/); [omnichannel-consistency](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/omnichannel-consistency/)) — which a design-system + tokens + lint enforces mechanically.

## 4. Prong C — Integration Into Our Agent Pipeline (MOST ACTIONABLE)

Our pipeline today (from `_FRAMEWORK_MVP_DoD.md`): `SPEC → BUILD → VALIDATE → [AUDIT if risk class] → MERGE GATE (Tier-1 mechanical + Tier-2 Integration Validator) → DONE`, with human gates only at the explicit §9 list (phase sign-off is item 3); `routine` UI gets "a light spec (acceptance criteria + test list)". The live frontend (`erp-workspace/frontend/src/ui/`) is 18 `App*` antd wrappers + one `theme/tokens.ts` (hardcoded hex), verified by behavioral vitest only. **Five concrete, by-construction mechanisms** map onto this without contradicting the doctrine:

### 4.1 UX-Discovery Spec Gate (enrich the existing UX-first law into a checklist)
Today PROJECT.md §5 mandates "UX-first" (problem → flow → scenarios before UI) but the spec "defines behavior, not visual design" — the founder's exact complaint. The fix is a **closed checklist the Spec Agent MUST answer for any UI-bearing stage before BUILD**, derived from §3. Industry validation that a precise spec converts the agent's job "from mind reading into pattern completion against a fixed target": GitHub **Spec Kit** ("intent is the source of truth"; a **constitution** file of governing principles incl. "user experience consistency") and Amazon **Kiro** (requirements in **EARS** notation — "WHEN [event] THE SYSTEM SHALL [behavior]" — "unambiguous and testable") ([github.blog Spec Kit](https://github.blog/ai-and-ml/generative-ai/spec-driven-development-with-ai-get-started-with-a-new-open-source-toolkit/); [kiro.dev/docs/specs](https://kiro.dev/docs/specs/)). The checklist (each item from a Prong-B source):
- **JTBD/flow:** What business event does this screen serve? Step-by-step user flow per task? Which role(s)?
- **IA/nav:** Where in the hierarchy (≤3 deep)? Sidebar item or in-page tab? Breadcrumb/current-location?
- **Tables:** Which of the 4 table tasks? Column order + alignment (numbers right)? Density? Sort? Bulk-select + batch bar? Pagination/virtualization? Sticky header + frozen ID column? **Empty/loading/error states?**
- **Forms:** Single-column? Labels above? Section grouping? Required/optional marking? Inline-validate-on-blur? Field-level error copy?
- **Filtering:** Instant or Apply-button? Chips + Clear-all? Facets vs simple?
- **CRUD:** Master-detail? Destructive-action confirmation copy + Undo? Type-to-confirm for high-severity?
- **Inline creation:** Which pickers need "+ Add new" (`popupRender`) + auto-select without leaving the form?
- **Comfort/discomfort (founder's framing):** What arrangement is most comfortable for this user's task? What would make it *uncomfortable* (too many clicks, hidden state, dense modal over reference data)?
- **Global conventions:** Which existing `App*` components + token groups must this reuse? What sibling screens must it stay consistent with?
- **Heuristics & a11y:** Passes the 10 heuristics (esp. system status, recognition-over-recall, expert accelerators)? WCAG 2.2 AA (POUR)?

Express the answerable, testable ones as **EARS acceptance criteria** so the Validator can check them (e.g. "WHEN the grid returns zero rows THE SYSTEM SHALL render the App empty-state"). **Effort: low-medium** (it is a spec-template + Spec-Agent-prompt edit + Phase-Architect acceptance-criteria requirement). This is the highest-leverage single change because it attacks the root cause the founder named: "the spec defines behavior but not visual design."

### 4.2 Mechanical fences (the "advisory → mechanical" upgrade; wire into Tier-1)
Every primary source converges: advisory prose degrades, a failing check does not ([Builder.io enforcement hierarchy](https://www.builder.io/blog/how-to-make-ai-agents-follow-your-design-system); [Anthropic — CLAUDE.md is "advisory," hooks are "deterministic"](https://code.claude.com/docs/en/best-practices)). We have the *targets* (the barrel, `tokens.ts`) but no *enforcement*. Add two ESLint rules, run in `scripts/test.sh` (Tier-1) so a violation fails the merge gate mechanically:
- **`no-restricted-imports`** blocking `antd` (and raw deep paths) anywhere outside `src/ui/` — enforces the "antd-fence" that is currently only convention. Builder.io's exact point: "An agent browsing your repo cannot tell `components/ui/` from `components/legacy/`" — only a failing lint stops it ([Builder.io](https://www.builder.io/blog/how-to-make-ai-agents-follow-your-design-system)).
- **A token rule** (`color-no-hex` style, cf. MetaMask's `eslint-plugin-design-tokens`, or `stylelint-declaration-strict-value`) failing on raw hex/spacing literals outside `src/ui/theme/` — makes "tokens as a contract" real ([MetaMask](https://github.com/MetaMask/eslint-plugin-design-tokens); [Atlassian ships this first-party](https://atlassian.design/components/eslint-plugin-design-system/); [stylelint-declaration-strict-value](https://github.com/AndyOGo/stylelint-declaration-strict-value)). Note our `tokens.ts` header already *claims* "no hex literals outside src/ui/theme/" — today that claim is unenforced; this rule makes it true.

**Effort: low** (two lint rules + wire into the existing test command). **This is the cheapest, most doctrine-aligned change** (mechanical guarantee, §20). Optionally add a Claude Code post-edit/Stop hook so a turn cannot end with violations.

### 4.3 Build-agent screenshot self-check (give BUILD a visual feedback loop)
Insert into the Builder role (or a bounded sub-step after BUILD self-verification): render the screen on the dev server, drive **Playwright MCP** to take an a11y snapshot + read console + screenshot, then run **one Claude vision pass against the closed rubric** from §2.3, capped at 2–3 fix iterations, retaining the best intermediate. Flags → escalate (not auto-fail). Backed by [arXiv 2604.05839](https://arxiv.org/html/2604.05839v1) (visual feedback ≈ +9–18%), [Anthropic best-practices](https://code.claude.com/docs/en/best-practices) (the recommended screenshot-compare pattern), [Playwright MCP](https://github.com/microsoft/playwright-mcp). **Effort: medium** (MCP wiring + a bounded loop + screenshot infra in the build worktree). **Honest caveat:** this catches *broken* UI autonomously; it does **not** certify *good* UI ([arXiv 2510.08783](https://arxiv.org/html/2510.08783v1)) — so it complements, never replaces, the human gate. Also adds token/wall-clock cost per UI stage; gate it to UI-bearing stages only.

### 4.4 Human visual-preview gate before sign-off (the taste arbiter, cheaply)
Every real autonomous team keeps a human on the merge gate for UI ([Stripe Minions](https://stripe.dev/blog/minions-stripes-one-shot-end-to-end-coding-agents): >1,300 AI PRs/week, all human-reviewed; [Shopify](https://www.bvp.com/atlas/inside-shopifys-ai-first-engineering-playbook): "not yet at the place where we allow AI to check in code automatically"; [Thoughtworks Radar v34](https://www.thoughtworks.com/about-us/news/2026/combat-ai-cognitive-debt-radar-v34): HOLD on "complacency with AI-generated code"). Today our founder's only UI touchpoint is the phase sign-off (§9 item 3), reading artifacts — he never *sees* the screens. Add a **per-change live preview the founder clicks through**, surfaced on the existing dashboard:
- **Cheapest, founder-operable path:** a **Vercel/Netlify-style preview deployment** of the frontend (point-and-click, comment directly on the live page, free tier) — but this **requires the frontend to be independently deployable** (see §4.6) and a reachable backend ([vercel.com/docs/comments](https://vercel.com/docs/comments); [Netlify Drawer](https://docs.netlify.com/deploy/review-deploys/netlify-drawer-for-feedback/overview/)). Given our Tailscale-only, "no new infrastructure class" dashboard constraint, the pragmatic first version is **a published static Storybook + the already-running ERP test instance** (we have one — see `erp-local-test-instance.md`) linked from the dashboard's existing phase-sign-off card, so the founder *looks* before approving.
- **Storybook** is the component review surface: each component in every state, side-by-side, on a URL "without touching code or requiring a local dev environment" ([storybook.js.org/docs/sharing/publish-storybook](https://storybook.js.org/docs/sharing/publish-storybook)).

**Effort: medium** (a preview/screenshot artifact per UI stage + a dashboard card; full per-PR Vercel preview is larger and depends on §4.6). **This is the one mechanism that judges taste** — the sources are unanimous that nothing else does, and `technical-context.md` already designates the founder as sole UI arbiter. **Protect his scarce time by front-loading §4.2/§4.3** so he only sees changes that already passed lint + visual self-check. The documented #1 AI-UI failure mode for a single human arbiter is **review throughput** (AI PRs face "2.5–5.3× longer review delays" — [secondtalent](https://www.secondtalent.com/resources/ai-generated-code-quality-metrics-and-statistics-for-2026/)); a point-and-click preview (vs reading diffs) is the mitigation.

### 4.5 Reference/mockup step (optional, before BUILD)
A cheap "golden screen" exemplar raises output quality via few-shot (Anthropic: "Examples are one of the most reliable ways to steer Claude's output" — relevant, diverse, structured; [multishot docs](https://docs.anthropic.com/en/docs/build-with-claude/prompt-engineering/multishot-prompting)). Two cheap forms: (a) the Spec Agent (or a v0 draft, used purely as a throwaway visual reference — *not* merged) produces a static mock the Builder screenshot-compares against (closes the §2.3 loop with a real target instead of "judge aesthetics"); (b) maintain 1–2 hand-blessed "golden" ERP screens in-repo as the canonical few-shot the Builder is always shown. **Effort: low-medium.** Skip v0 if the lock-in/credential overhead isn't worth it; the in-repo golden-screen approach is free and needs no vendor.

### 4.6 Backend/frontend as DISTINCT build stages (makes the frontend cheap & low-risk)
Today a stage builds both backend and frontend; the founder wants the frontend "cheap and low-risk to change." This is the canonical decoupling argument: a separate frontend pipeline "reduces the scope of any given deployment, which in turn reduces the associated risk" ([Martin Fowler, Micro Frontends](https://martinfowler.com/articles/micro-frontends.html)); the API contract is the safe boundary, mechanically enforced via consumer-driven contract tests / **Pact `can-i-deploy`** so independent deploys don't become "a production surprise" ([martinfowler.com/articles/consumerDrivenContracts](https://martinfowler.com/articles/consumerDrivenContracts.html); [docs.pact.io](https://docs.pact.io/pact_broker/can_i_deploy)). **Right-sizing (important honesty):** full micro-frontends/BFF are for multiple teams/clients — overkill for us; "for a single-UI ERP you get ~all the benefit from one frontend build unit + one backend build unit joined by a tested API contract. Skip BFF until a mobile app or partner API exists" ([samnewman.io/BFF](https://samnewman.io/patterns/architectural/bff/)). For us this means: **decompose UI-bearing stages so the frontend slice is its own stage** (or sub-stage) against the frozen API/cross-phase contracts we *already* produce (C1–C4) — which fits D-0050's "finer stages" directive perfectly. Benefits: UI churn can't break backend; UI re-themes/iterations are low-blast-radius; and it is the prerequisite that makes the §4.4 preview gate cheap. **Effort: medium** (a Phase-Architect decomposition convention + ensuring the FE slice builds/tests against the contract independently). **Cost:** more, smaller stages (we already want this) and the contract check must be wired into Tier-1 (largely is, via the Integration Validator + contracts).

### 4.7 Config-driven tokens (so the founder can re-theme centrally)
`technical-context.md` requires "a visual change propagates from that one place," and `tokens.ts` is that place — but its values are **hardcoded hex in code**, so only an agent can change them. To honor "the founder is the UI arbiter," make the token *values* a config artifact (JSON, DTCG-shaped) the `ConfigProvider` reads, so a palette/spacing change is a config edit — not a code change requiring a full stage ([W3C DTCG](https://www.designtokens.org/tr/drafts/format/); [Style Dictionary single-source](https://styledictionary.com/info/dtcg/); Ant Design theming is natively token-based — [customize-theme](https://ant.design/docs/react/customize-theme)). This also aligns with our Doctrine §14 ("everything configurable = configurable; values = config, not hardcoded"). **Effort: low.** **Honest limit:** tokens guarantee *consistency*, never *taste* — "technically correct but devoid of specific identity" ([builtin.com](https://builtin.com/articles/ai-design-slop-era)); re-theming gives the founder a steering wheel, not an autopilot.

## 6. Open Questions for the Founder (plain language)

1. **How much do you want to look, and where?** The cheapest "you see it before it's done" is: after each screen is built, the dashboard shows you a link to the live screen on the test ERP (and a gallery of every button/table state). You click through and approve or send it back. Is that enough, or do you want a screenshot pasted right into the dashboard card so you don't even click? (This decides how much we build for recommendation #4.)

2. **Re-theming control — how hands-on?** We can make all colors/spacing/fonts a single settings file you (or we, on your word) can change in one place, and every screen updates. Do you want to actually adjust these yourself, or just be able to say "make it warmer / more compact" and have an agent apply it centrally? (Decides the shape of #3.)

3. **"Tasteful" — can you give us 1–2 reference apps you like?** Taste is the one thing the machine can't invent (the research is unanimous). If you point at 1–2 existing apps/screens whose look you like (even a competitor, or 1C done right), we turn that into a "golden screen" the agents copy the feel of. Without a reference, the agent averages its training data — which is exactly the "tasteless" you're seeing. (Feeds #2 and #7.)

4. **Where is your time best spent reviewing?** You're the only UI judge and your time is the bottleneck. We'll auto-catch broken/inconsistent screens before they reach you. Of what's left, do you want to review *every* screen, or only the important ones (the documents you live in — cont de plată, ZN verification) and let routine list screens pass on automated checks alone? (Decides whether the human gate is on all UI stages or only high-traffic ones.)

5. **Accessibility target.** Industry default is "WCAG AA" (keyboard-usable, sufficient contrast, screen-reader labels). For an internal tool used by your staff this is cheap to bake in and improves everyday usability (contrast, keyboard speed). Confirm we should hold the agents to AA by default. (Decides a spec-gate + lint item.)
